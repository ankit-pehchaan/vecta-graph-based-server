COMMON AUSTRALIAN FINANCIAL LIFE STAGE SCENARIOS

Young Professional (25-30):
- Typical concerns: HECS/HELP debt, saving for first home, starting career
- Common goals: pay off HELP debt, build emergency fund, first home purchase
- Key considerations: FHSS scheme, first home buyer grants, employer super
- Typical income: $55,000-$85,000
- Common mistake: ignoring super and insurance at this age

Young Family (30-40):
- Typical concerns: mortgage, child care costs, one income vs two
- Common goals: pay down mortgage, child education planning, insurance review
- Key considerations: child care subsidy, negative gearing on investment property,
  salary sacrifice to super, income protection while earning peak income
- Typical household income: $120,000-$200,000
- Common mistake: under-insured, no estate planning, not reviewing super

Established Family (40-50):
- Typical concerns: teenagers approaching uni, aging parents, career peak
- Common goals: wealth accumulation, child education funding, retirement planning
- Key considerations: maximise super contributions (catch-up contributions from
  2019-20), investment diversification, estate planning, aged care for parents
- Typical household income: $150,000-$300,000
- Common mistake: lifestyle inflation, not enough super, ignoring aged care planning

Pre-Retirement (50-60):
- Typical concerns: retirement readiness, downsizing, health costs
- Common goals: maximise super, reduce debt, plan retirement income stream
- Key considerations: transition to retirement (TTR), downsizer contribution
  ($300,000 each if 55+), preservation age access, asset allocation shift
- Typical super balance: $300,000-$700,000 (wide variance)
- Common mistake: too conservative too early, not planning healthcare costs

Retirement (60+):
- Typical concerns: income sustainability, healthcare, aged care, legacy
- Common goals: comfortable retirement lifestyle, estate planning, aged care prep
- Key considerations: age pension eligibility, account-based pension drawdown,
  aged care costs and funding, estate planning and testamentary trusts
- Common mistake: drawdown too fast, not considering longevity risk

Single Parent:
- Typical concerns: sole income, child care costs, housing affordability
- Key benefits: Family Home Guarantee (2% deposit), Parenting Payment,
  Family Tax Benefit, child care subsidy
- Common goals: financial stability, emergency fund, child education
- Key considerations: insurance (critical as sole provider), Centrelink benefits

Career Changer:
- Typical concerns: income gap during transition, retraining costs, super gap
- Common goals: build buffer for transition, maintain insurance during gap
- Key considerations: impact on super contributions, HELP debt for retraining,
  income protection insurance validity during career change

Small Business Owner:
- Typical concerns: irregular income, business vs personal finances, super
- Common goals: business growth, personal wealth separate from business,
  retirement planning (often neglected)
- Key considerations: not eligible for SG (must make own super contributions),
  small business CGT concessions, income protection structure,
  business succession planning

Property Investor:
- Typical concerns: cash flow, vacancy risk, interest rate exposure
- Common goals: portfolio growth, positive cash flow, tax optimisation
- Key considerations: negative gearing strategy, CGT planning, land tax,
  diversification (not all eggs in property basket), interest rate buffers

Expat Returning to Australia:
- Typical concerns: foreign assets, tax residency, super contributions gap
- Common goals: reintegrate finances, catch up on super, property purchase
- Key considerations: foreign income tax treatment, super catch-up
  contributions, CGT on foreign assets, health insurance waiting periods
