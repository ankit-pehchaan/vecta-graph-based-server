AUSTRALIAN PROPERTY - KEY FACTS

First Home Buyer Schemes:
- First Home Owner Grant (FHOG): varies by state, typically $10,000-$30,000
  for new homes. Not available for established homes in most states.
- First Home Super Saver Scheme (FHSS): withdraw voluntary super contributions
  for deposit (max $50,000 total)
- First Home Guarantee: purchase with 5% deposit, no LMI, limited places
  per year. Income caps apply ($125,000 single, $200,000 couple).
- Regional First Home Buyer Guarantee: similar but for regional areas
- Family Home Guarantee: single parents with dependents, 2% deposit

Stamp Duty by State (approximate, subject to change):
- NSW: First home buyer exemption up to $800,000 (new) / $650,000 (existing)
  Stamp duty replaced by annual property tax option for first home buyers
- VIC: First home buyer exemption up to $600,000, concession to $750,000
- QLD: First home concession up to $550,000, exemption for first home owners
- WA: First home buyer exemption up to $430,000 (vacant land $300,000)
- SA: No stamp duty for first home buyers up to $650,000

Cultural Context:
- Property ownership runs deep in Australian financial planning culture
- "Bricks and mortar" preference: many Australians view property as the
  most tangible and trusted asset class
- Negative gearing + 50% CGT discount make AU property uniquely
  tax-advantaged compared to other countries
- Average Australian mortgage: approximately $600,000 (varies by city)
- Median house prices: Sydney ~$1.4M, Melbourne ~$950k, Brisbane ~$850k

Investment Property:
- Negative Gearing: claim rental property losses against personal income
  Interest, depreciation, repairs, management fees all deductible
- Capital Gains Tax (CGT): 50% discount if held 12+ months
  Not available for companies. Applies to individuals and trusts.
- Depreciation: building allowance (2.5% of construction cost for post-1987)
  Plant and equipment depreciation (diminishing value or prime cost)
- Land Tax: annual tax on investment properties (varies by state)
  Primary residence exempt in all states
- LVR (Loan-to-Value Ratio): critical for borrowing capacity
  Most lenders require 80% LVR max to avoid Lenders Mortgage Insurance
  Existing equity in home can be used for investment property deposit
  Serviceability: lenders assess ability to repay at higher buffer rates

Offset Accounts:
- Offset accounts reduce mortgage interest while keeping funds liquid
- Common Australian strategy: effectively earns the mortgage interest
  rate tax-free on savings held in the offset
- 100% offset: every dollar in offset reduces the loan balance for
  interest calculation purposes
- Key planning tool: offset vs redraw, offset vs separate savings

Rental Yield:
- Gross yield = annual rent / property value
- Australian average gross yield: 3-5% depending on location
- Capital cities typically lower yield, higher growth
- Regional areas typically higher yield, lower growth

Property Investment Structures:
- Individual: simplest, negative gearing available
- Joint tenants: equal ownership, automatic survivorship
- Tenants in common: flexible ownership splits
- Company: no CGT discount, flat 25-30% tax rate
- Trust (discretionary): flexibility in income distribution
- SMSF: strict rules, limited recourse borrowing arrangements (LRBA)
