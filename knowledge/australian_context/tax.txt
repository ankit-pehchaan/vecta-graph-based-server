AUSTRALIAN TAX - KEY FACTS

Income Tax Brackets (2024-25):
- $0 - $18,200: Nil
- $18,201 - $45,000: 16 cents per dollar over $18,200
- $45,001 - $135,000: $4,288 + 30 cents per dollar over $45,000
- $135,001 - $190,000: $31,288 + 37 cents per dollar over $135,000
- $190,001+: $51,638 + 45 cents per dollar over $190,000
- Medicare Levy: additional 2% on taxable income

Tax Offsets:
- Low Income Tax Offset (LITO): up to $700 for incomes up to $45,000
  Reduces to zero at $66,668
- Low and Middle Income Tax Offset (LMITO): ended 30 June 2022
- Senior Australians and Pensioners Tax Offset (SAPTO)
- Private Health Insurance Rebate (income-tested)

Deductions:
- Work-related expenses: must be directly related to earning income
- Working from home: fixed rate 67 cents per hour (from 2022-23)
  or actual cost method
- Self-education: if related to current employment
- Charitable donations: to DGR-registered organisations
- Investment property expenses: interest, depreciation, repairs, management
- Income protection insurance premiums

Capital Gains Tax (CGT):
- CGT discount: 50% for individuals on assets held 12+ months
- No CGT on main residence (primary home)
- CGT applies to: investment property, shares, crypto, collectables
- Net capital loss: can be carried forward indefinitely
- Small business CGT concessions available

Negative Gearing:
- Claim investment losses against other income (salary, business)
- Common with property: rental income < expenses = tax loss
- Expenses include: interest, depreciation, repairs, insurance, management
- Effectively reduces marginal tax rate on investment

Family Trusts:
- Discretionary trust: distribute income to beneficiaries at lower tax rates
- Can split income among family members (including adult children)
- Minor beneficiaries: unearned income taxed at penalty rates above $416
- Not as effective since Stage 3 tax cuts (lower marginal rates)
- Setup cost: $1,500-$3,000, annual compliance: $1,000-$2,000

PAYG Instalments:
- Pre-payment of tax on investment and business income
- Quarterly or annual depending on income level
- Can vary instalment amount if income changes

Tax Planning Strategies (Educational, not advice):
- Salary sacrifice to super (reduce taxable income, boost retirement)
- Timing of deductions (prepay expenses before 30 June)
- Spouse contribution splitting (super)
- Structure investments tax-effectively (individual vs trust vs company)
- Maximise deductions: keep receipts, claim all eligible expenses
