AUSTRALIAN CENTRELINK / GOVERNMENT BENEFITS - KEY FACTS

Family Tax Benefit (FTB):
- Part A: per child payment, income-tested
  Base rate + supplement for each child under 20 (or 16-19 in full-time study)
  Income test: reduces after $58,108 family income
- Part B: per family payment for single-income or low-income families
  Paid per family (not per child)
  Primary earner income test: $100,900 threshold
  Secondary earner: up to $6,059 before reduction

Parenting Payment:
- Single: up to $922.10 per fortnight (income-tested)
  Youngest child under 14
- Partnered: up to $631.80 per fortnight (income-tested)
  Youngest child under 6

Child Care Subsidy (CCS):
- Up to 90% of child care fees (income-tested)
- Family income under $80,000: 90% subsidy
- Reduces by 1% for every $5,000 over $80,000
- Activity test: hours of recognised activity per fortnight
- Annual cap removed for families earning under $530,000

JobSeeker Payment:
- Single, no children: ~$762.70 per fortnight
- Single, with children: ~$816.90 per fortnight
- Partnered: ~$693.10 per fortnight
- Income test: $150 per fortnight before reduction
- Assets test applies
- Mutual obligation requirements (job search, activities)

Carer Payment:
- For people providing constant care to someone with disability/illness
- Rate: equivalent to Age Pension (~$1,116.30 per fortnight single)
- Income and assets tested
- Carer Allowance: additional supplement (~$144.80 per fortnight)

Disability Support Pension (DSP):
- For people with permanent disability who cannot work 15+ hours per week
- Rate: equivalent to Age Pension
- Income and assets tested
- Impairment tables assessment required

Commonwealth Rent Assistance:
- Additional payment for those receiving eligible Centrelink payments
- Minimum rent threshold before assistance starts
- Maximum rate: ~$188.20 per fortnight (single, no children)
- Paid on top of primary Centrelink payment

Paid Parental Leave:
- Government-funded: 20 weeks at national minimum wage (~$882.75/week)
- Increasing to 26 weeks by July 2026
- Income test: $168,865 individual income in previous financial year
- Can be shared between eligible parents
- Employer-funded parental leave is separate and additional

Bereavement Payments:
- Lump sum and ongoing payments available after death of partner
- Varies by payment type
- Transition period for pension rate adjustments
