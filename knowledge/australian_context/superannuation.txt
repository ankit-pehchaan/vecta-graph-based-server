AUSTRALIAN SUPERANNUATION - KEY FACTS

Superannuation Guarantee (SG):
- Employer contribution rate: 12% of Ordinary Time Earnings (as of 2025-26)
- Scheduled to reach 12% from 1 July 2025 (was 11.5% in 2024-25)
- Applies to employees earning $450+ per month (threshold removed from July 2022)

Contribution Caps (2024-25):
- Concessional (before-tax) cap: $30,000 per year
  Includes employer SG, salary sacrifice, personal deductible contributions
- Non-concessional (after-tax) cap: $120,000 per year
  Bring-forward rule: up to $360,000 over 3 years if under 75

Preservation Age:
- Born before 1 July 1960: age 55
- Born 1 July 1960 to 30 June 1961: age 56
- Born 1 July 1961 to 30 June 1962: age 57
- Born 1 July 1962 to 30 June 1963: age 58
- Born 1 July 1963 to 30 June 1964: age 59
- Born after 30 June 1964: age 60

Access Conditions:
- Reached preservation age and permanently retired
- Reached age 60 and ceased employment
- Reached age 65 (unrestricted access)
- Severe financial hardship or compassionate grounds (limited)
- Terminal medical condition
- Temporary residents departing Australia permanently

Super Fund Types:
- Industry funds: typically lower fees, run for members
- Retail funds: run by banks/financial institutions
- Self-Managed Super Funds (SMSF): self-directed, min 1 max 6 members
  SMSF requirements: separate bank account, annual audit, investment strategy
- Employer/corporate funds: some large employers offer their own

Tax Treatment:
- Contributions tax: 15% on concessional contributions
- Earnings tax: 15% on investment earnings within super
- Division 293 tax: additional 15% for individuals earning $250,000+
- Tax-free on withdrawal after age 60 (from taxed fund)
- Transition to Retirement (TTR): access super while still working after preservation age

Spouse Contributions:
- Tax offset of up to $540 for contributions to low-income spouse
- Spouse must earn less than $40,000 p.a. for maximum offset
- Contribution splitting: transfer up to 85% of concessional contributions to spouse

Co-contribution:
- Government matches personal non-concessional contributions
- Maximum $500 government co-contribution for $1,000 personal contribution
- Income threshold: $43,445 to $58,445 (2024-25)

First Home Super Saver Scheme (FHSS):
- Voluntary contributions to super can be withdrawn for first home deposit
- Maximum $15,000 per financial year, $50,000 total
- Includes associated earnings (deemed rate)
- Must not have previously owned property in Australia
