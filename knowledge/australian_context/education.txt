AUSTRALIAN EDUCATION COSTS - KEY FACTS

HECS/HELP (Higher Education Loan Program):
- Government loan for university tuition fees
- No upfront payment required; debt repaid through tax system
- Repayment threshold: $54,435 (2024-25), repayment rates 1-10% of income
- Indexed annually to CPI (not interest-bearing in traditional sense)
- Voluntary repayments can reduce balance faster
- No discount for upfront payment (removed in 2017)

University Costs (approximate annual):
- Commonwealth Supported Place (CSP): $4,000-$16,000 depending on course
  Band 1 (humanities, social science): ~$4,000-$4,500
  Band 2 (science, engineering, health): ~$8,000-$9,500
  Band 3 (law, commerce, dentistry): ~$14,500-$16,000
- Full-fee places: $20,000-$50,000+ per year
- Student contribution amounts set by government, updated annually

TAFE / Vocational Education:
- Certificate III/IV: $2,000-$8,000
- Diploma: $5,000-$15,000
- VET Student Loans available for diploma and above
- Apprenticeships: employer-funded training, earn while you learn

Private School Costs:
- Primary: $10,000-$25,000 per year
- Secondary: $15,000-$40,000+ per year
- Top private schools in Sydney/Melbourne: $35,000-$45,000+
- Boarding fees additional: $15,000-$30,000 per year
- Annual increases typically 3-5% above inflation

Catholic/Independent Schools:
- Catholic systemic: $2,000-$8,000 per year
- Catholic independent: $10,000-$25,000 per year
- Generally more affordable than non-denominational private

Public Schools:
- No tuition fees (state-funded)
- Voluntary contributions: $200-$500 per year
- Additional costs: uniforms, excursions, technology levy

Child Care Costs:
- Long day care: $100-$200+ per day depending on area
- Child Care Subsidy (CCS): up to 90% of fees, income-tested
  Activity test applies (hours of work/study)
- Annual cap removed from July 2023 for families earning under $530,000

Education Savings Options:
- Dedicated savings account (no specific tax advantage in Australia)
- Investment bonds: tax-paid after 10 years, no personal tax on withdrawal
- Family trust distributions
- Salary sacrifice to super then FHSS (not applicable for education)
- Regular investment plan (ETFs, managed funds)

- HECS/HELP covers tuition fees ONLY â€” it does NOT cover living costs
- Average university living costs in Australia: $20,000-$25,000 per year
  in capital cities (rent, food, transport, textbooks, utilities)
- Regional students moving to city face higher costs (accommodation)
- Property can serve as student housing or income source during uni years
- Many parents want to support children through uni to avoid
  excessive part-time work affecting academic performance

Key Planning Considerations:
- Start early: compound growth over 10-18 years makes a significant difference
- Children aged 12 are approximately 5 years from university
- Factor in living costs for university (rent, food, transport if away from home)
- Consider gap year savings by the child
- Part-time work by student can supplement
- Scholarship opportunities (academic, sporting, community)
